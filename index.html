Computer components

  • Case and form factors

    • "form factor" refers to the standardized size, shape, and physical specifications of hardware components like motherboards, cases, and power supplies, ensuring compatibility and interchangeability

  • Motherboard

    • The main circuit board that holds key components and allows other parts to connect and communicate.

  • BOI/UEFI

    • .Firmware that starts the computer, manages hardware, and helps the operating system communicate with it.

  • Memory

    • Stores data and instructions for quick access by the processor to keep the system running smoothly.

  • Processors

    • The central unit that carries out instructions and calculations, functioning as the "brain" of the computer.

  • Video and Expantion cards

    • Expansion cards improve a computer’s capabilities, with video cards specifically handling graphics.

  • Cooling

    • Helps prevent overheating by dissipating heat from computer components.

  • Power Suply

    • Converts electricity from the wall into a usable form for the computer’s components.

What is Networking?

Welcome to Computer Networking

Networking connects computers to share information and work together.

  • Components: Things like routers and switches help connect devices.
  • Addresses: Computers use special addresses like IPs to talk to each other.
  • Benefits: Share files, use the internet, and connect devices easily.
  • Types: Examples include small networks (LAN) and big ones (WAN).
